## What it is

"Fat burners" or thermogenics are stimulant-based blends sold to boost
metabolism and strip fat. In practice they are mostly caffeine wrapped in
marketing, and no supplement overrides the need for an energy deficit.

## What the evidence shows

**Burning fat.** <EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" basis="human" />
Most of any real effect is the caffeine, a mild, temporary bump in metabolic
rate and appetite suppression. The other ingredients are typically underdosed or
unproven, and none replace an overall energy deficit.

## Who it suits

There is little case for them. What actually drives fat loss is a sustained
energy deficit, enough protein, resistance training to keep muscle, and daily
movement, none of which a pill can substitute for.

## Practical notes

The category has a chequered safety record: high stimulant loads, hidden doses,
and historically banned or withdrawn ingredients. Stacking a fat burner with
coffee or a pre-workout risks jitters, a racing heart and poor sleep. If you use
caffeine as a tool, our [caffeine calculator](/caffeine-calculator) helps you
manage the total. People who are pregnant or have heart, blood-pressure or
anxiety conditions should avoid them.
