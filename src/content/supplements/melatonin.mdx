## What it is

Melatonin is a hormone that signals "time for sleep" to your body clock. That
makes it a body-clock tool more than a sedative — a distinction that explains
where it works well and where it disappoints.

## What the evidence shows

**Jet lag and body-clock timing.** <EvidenceTier tier="well-supported" basis="human" />
Its strongest evidence is for shifting the body clock — jet lag and delayed
sleep timing.

**As a general sleeping aid.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
It can modestly help you fall asleep a bit faster, but it is not a powerful
sleeping pill, and its long-term nightly use is not well studied.

## Who it suits

Travellers crossing time zones and people whose sleep timing has drifted late —
more than someone simply after a stronger nightly sedative.

## Practical notes

Timing often matters more than amount: because it signals your body clock, when
you take it is important, and lower amounts at the right time can work as well as
larger ones. It is a hormone, so avoid it in pregnancy and seek medical advice
for children or ongoing use — and note it is prescription-only in some countries.
