## What it is

Whey is one of the two proteins in milk, sold as a convenient powder. Nutritionally
it is simply a high-quality, fast-digesting protein source, a food in powder
form, not a special muscle-building compound.

## What the evidence shows

**Supporting muscle when protein intake is short.** <EvidenceTier tier="well-supported" basis="human" />
The well-established finding, reflected in the ISSN position stand, is that
**total daily protein** drives muscle retention and growth. Protein powder helps
by making it easier to hit that target, its benefit is real but comes from the
protein, not from being a powder or from any brand.

**Extra benefit once your target is already met.** <EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" />
Piling on more protein beyond your daily needs doesn't add further muscle. If you
already reach your target from food, powder is convenience, not a performance
edge.

**Whey vs plant protein.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
Whey is complete and leucine-rich; good plant blends can match it when total
protein and leucine are adequate. Differences are small for most people.

## Who it suits

People who struggle to reach their protein target from whole food, or who want a
quick, portable option around training or a busy day. If your diet already
delivers enough protein, you don't need it.

## Practical notes

Aim for roughly **1.6 to 2.2 g of protein per kg of bodyweight per day** for muscle
goals, spread across meals; powder fills any gap. Work out your target with the
[macro calculator](/macro-calculator) and total needs with the
[TDEE calculator](/tdee-calculator).
