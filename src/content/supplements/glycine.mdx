## What it is

Glycine is a simple amino acid found in food, taken before bed for sleep
quality. Unlike melatonin it is not a hormone and doesn't shift your body clock;
the two work by different routes.

## What the evidence shows

**Sleep quality.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
A few small studies suggest taking glycine before bed can improve subjective
sleep quality and reduce next-day tiredness, possibly by gently lowering core
body temperature. Promising, but based on limited research.

## Who it suits

People after a gentle, well-tolerated sleep aid who want something other than a
hormone like melatonin.

## Practical notes

Glycine is generally very well tolerated at the amounts studied. As with most
sleep supplements, expectations should be modest — the foundations of good sleep
matter more, and our [sleep calculator](/sleep-calculator) helps you set a
sensible schedule.
