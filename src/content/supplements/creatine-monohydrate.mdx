## What it is

Creatine is a compound your body already makes and stores in muscle, where it
helps rapidly regenerate ATP, the fast energy currency for short, hard efforts.
Supplementing raises muscle creatine stores above what diet alone provides.
Creatine monohydrate is the original, cheapest and by far the most-studied form.

## What the evidence shows

**Strength, power and muscle.** <EvidenceTier tier="well-supported" basis="human" />
This is as strong as supplement evidence gets. Across a large body of human
trials, summarised in the ISSN position stand, creatine reliably improves
high-intensity performance and, combined with resistance training, increases
strength and lean mass. It works by improving the quality of your training over
weeks, not as an acute stimulant.

**Cognition and other uses.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
There is growing but earlier evidence for benefits to cognition (especially
under sleep deprivation) and other areas. Interesting, not yet settled.

**Fat loss or "toning" on its own.** <EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" />
Creatine doesn't burn fat. Any body-composition benefit comes indirectly, via
supporting harder training and more muscle.

## Who it suits

Anyone doing resistance or high-intensity training who wants a well-evidenced,
low-cost edge. Vegetarians and vegans often see a slightly larger effect, as
their baseline muscle creatine tends to be lower.

## Practical notes

Loading is optional, a standard daily amount saturates muscle within a few
weeks just as well, with less chance of stomach upset. Timing doesn't matter
much; consistency does. Stick to plain **monohydrate**: the pricier "advanced"
forms haven't been shown to beat it. Our
