## What it is

Magnesium is an essential mineral involved in hundreds of processes, including
muscle and nerve function and sleep. Mild shortfalls are fairly common, which is
where supplementing can have a role.

## What the evidence shows

**Fixing a deficiency (sleep, cramps).** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
If you're genuinely low, correcting magnesium may help sleep quality and muscle
cramps. The evidence is stronger for people who were short to begin with than for
everyone.

**A benefit when you're already replete.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
Topping up someone who already gets enough has not been shown to reliably improve
sleep, performance or recovery. Much of the "magnesium for sleep" marketing
overstates this.

**Strength and testosterone claims.** <EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" />
Not supported beyond, again, correcting a deficiency.

## Who it suits

People with a likely shortfall (low intake of greens, nuts, seeds, legumes and
wholegrains) or troubled sleep worth investigating. Food first; supplement to
fill a real gap.

## Practical notes

Choose a **well-absorbed form** (citrate, glycinate or malate) over magnesium
oxide, which absorbs poorly and is the most likely to cause loose stools (see the
safety box). It pairs conventionally with zinc in
[ZMA](/supplements/zma).
