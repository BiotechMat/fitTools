## What it is

Zinc is an essential mineral involved in immunity, hormone production and many
enzymes. As with most minerals, correcting a genuine shortfall helps, but more
is not better.

## What the evidence shows

**Testosterone.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
Zinc's role here is mainly about correcting a deficiency. In men who are
genuinely low, restoring it can help hormone levels; in men who already have
enough, extra zinc does not reliably raise testosterone despite the marketing.

**Colds.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
There is reasonable evidence that zinc taken promptly at the onset of a cold may
shorten it slightly, though results vary by form and dose.

## Who it suits

People with a likely or tested shortfall, some athletes, and those with limited
dietary sources. Meat, shellfish, seeds and legumes cover most people's needs.

## Practical notes

Chronic high doses cause a copper deficiency, which can lead to anaemia and
nerve problems, so don't take a lot long-term without a reason. Large single
doses on an empty stomach commonly cause nausea.
