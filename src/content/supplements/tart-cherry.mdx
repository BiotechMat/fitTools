## What it is

Tart (Montmorency) cherry is a polyphenol- and anthocyanin-rich fruit, usually
taken as a concentrate or juice. It is used around hard training and competition
for recovery and, more tentatively, for sleep.

## What the evidence shows

**Recovery and muscle soreness.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
There is promising evidence that its polyphenols can reduce soreness and speed
recovery after hard or damaging exercise, which is why some endurance athletes
use it around events.

**Sleep.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
Some small studies report modest improvements in sleep, possibly via its
melatonin content. The effect is gentle rather than a sleeping pill.

## Who it suits

Endurance athletes and anyone needing to recover quickly between closely-spaced
hard sessions or competition rounds.

## Practical notes

Like other high-dose antioxidants, routinely blunting exercise-induced
inflammation could dampen some training adaptations, so many use it around
competition rather than every day. Pair recovery supplements with the basics —
sleep, protein and sensible load management, which you can track with our
[recovery-readiness index](/recovery-readiness-index).
