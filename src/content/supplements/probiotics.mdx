## What it is

Probiotics are live bacteria taken to support the gut. The crucial point is that
"probiotics" is a huge category of different strains doing different things — a
benefit shown for one named strain says little about an unrelated one in another
product.

## What the evidence shows

**Gut and immune outcomes in athletes.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
Some strains have preliminary evidence for gut comfort, reducing the frequency
or duration of colds, and supporting the gut during heavy training. Benefits are
strain-specific.

## Who it suits

People targeting a specific, studied strain for a specific reason — not everyone
looking for a general "gut health" boost.

## Practical notes

Because effects don't generalise, the label detail matters: a product without a
named, studied strain and dose may do nothing. Fermented foods like yoghurt,
kefir and sauerkraut supply live cultures alongside a broader diet-quality
benefit, and are a sensible first port of call. People who are seriously
immunocompromised should only use probiotics under medical guidance.
