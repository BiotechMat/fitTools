## What it is

Beta-alanine is an amino acid that combines with histidine to form carnosine in
muscle. Carnosine buffers the hydrogen ions (acidity) that build up during hard
effort and contribute to fatigue. Supplementing raises muscle carnosine over
time.

## What the evidence shows

**High-intensity efforts of ~1 to 4 minutes.** <EvidenceTier tier="well-supported" basis="human" />
The ISSN position stand supports a **small but real** improvement in exercise
lasting roughly one to four minutes, the window where acid buffering matters
most, such as hard intervals or high-rep sets to failure.

**Single maximal lifts and long endurance.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
Little reason to expect much here, the mechanism doesn't fit a single 1RM
attempt or steady long-distance work. Evidence outside the 1 to 4 minute window is
weak.

**The tingle meaning it's working.** <EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" />
The skin tingle (paraesthesia) is just a harmless response to a larger single
dose. It is not a marker of effect, and it disappears if you split the dose.

## Who it suits

People doing repeated high-intensity intervals or high-rep resistance work.
Less relevant for pure strength or steady endurance athletes.

## Practical notes

It works by **loading carnosine over several weeks** of consistent daily use, so
timing around a workout is irrelevant, miss the "pre-workout" framing. Splitting
the daily amount into smaller doses avoids the tingle. It stacks sensibly with
[creatine](/supplements/creatine-monohydrate).
