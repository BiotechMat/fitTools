## What it is

BCAAs are three of the essential amino acids (leucine, isoleucine and valine)
sold as a muscle-building and recovery aid. The catch is that building muscle
needs all the essential amino acids, and complete proteins already contain
plenty of these three.

## What the evidence shows

**Muscle growth and recovery.** <EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" basis="human" />
For anyone eating enough total protein, BCAAs add very little. The three
branched-chain amino acids alone provide an incomplete signal for building
muscle, and whole proteins like whey supply them anyway.

## Who it suits

For a healthy person hitting their protein target, they are largely redundant.
The clearest genuine uses are certain clinical situations, not everyday
training.

## Practical notes

A serving of complete protein (whey, or a good meal) does the same job more
completely and usually more cheaply. Focus on total daily protein instead; the
[macro calculator](/macro-calculator) helps you set a target. BCAAs are mostly a
solution to a problem most trainees don't have.
