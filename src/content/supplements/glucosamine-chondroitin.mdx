## What it is

Glucosamine and chondroitin are components of cartilage, sold — often together —
as joint supplements. Glucosamine is frequently derived from shellfish, so
people with a shellfish allergy should check the source.

## What the evidence shows

**Joint pain and osteoarthritis.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
The evidence is genuinely mixed. Large trials have conflicted: some people with
osteoarthritis report modest pain relief, while well-controlled studies often
show little better than placebo. It is a reasonable trial-and-see, not a sure
thing.

**Prevention in healthy joints.** <EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" />
There is little evidence they prevent joint problems or benefit healthy joints;
most research is in people who already have osteoarthritis.

## Who it suits

People with existing joint pain who want to test whether they are among the
responders, rather than athletes using it preventively.

## Practical notes

If someone responds, it typically takes several weeks. If there is no change
after a couple of months, continuing is unlikely to help. It can interact with
blood-thinning medication and may affect blood-sugar control, so seek advice if
either applies.
