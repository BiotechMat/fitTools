## What it is

Citrulline is an amino acid that the body converts to arginine, raising nitric
oxide and widening blood vessels, the basis of the "pump". It is often sold
bonded to malate (citrulline malate) and is a staple pre-workout ingredient.

## What the evidence shows

**Training volume and soreness.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
Some trials report a few extra reps before failure or reduced next-day soreness;
others find nothing. The picture is genuinely **mixed**, promising enough to be
worth a mention, not strong enough to promise results.

**The "pump".** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
Citrulline does reliably raise nitric oxide and blood flow, so the acute pump is
real. Whether that pump translates into more actual muscle growth is unproven.

**Endurance and fat loss.** <EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" />
Claims beyond short, hard resistance work (endurance gains, fat loss) aren't
supported by good human evidence.

## Who it suits

Lifters curious about a low-risk "might help a little" ingredient for high-rep
training, who won't be disappointed if the effect is subtle or absent.

## Practical notes

Doses in studies are often **higher than what many pre-workouts provide**, so
check the label rather than assuming a blend contains a useful amount.
Interestingly, citrulline raises blood arginine more effectively than taking
arginine itself. Pairs conventionally with
[caffeine](/supplements/caffeine); track your sets with the
[training volume calculator](/training-volume-calculator).
