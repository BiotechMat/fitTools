## What it is

Taurine is an amino acid found in the body and in many energy drinks. Despite
that association, it is not a stimulant, any kick from an energy drink comes
from the caffeine and sugar, not the taurine.

## What the evidence shows

**Endurance and recovery.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
Some studies suggest small improvements in endurance and a possible reduction in
muscle damage, but results are inconsistent and effect sizes are modest. It is a
reasonable "might help a little", not a reliable ergogenic.

## Who it suits

Endurance athletes curious about a low-risk, modest experiment. Expectations
should be measured. The marketing outstrips the fairly small evidence base.

## Practical notes

Taurine is generally well tolerated at the amounts studied, and the body also
makes it. As with most single ingredients, it is not a substitute for the
fundamentals of training, fuelling and recovery.
