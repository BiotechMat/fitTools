## What it is

Vitamin C is an essential nutrient and antioxidant. Deficiency is rare on a
varied diet, and for most well-nourished people high-dose supplements do little.

## What the evidence shows

**Preventing colds.** <EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" basis="human" />
For most people, regular supplements don't prevent colds, though they may
slightly shorten one. The famous claims greatly overstate a modest,
inconsistent effect.

**High-dose antioxidants and training.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
Routinely megadosing vitamin C (and E) around training may blunt some of the
adaptations you are training for, because a degree of exercise-induced oxidative
stress is part of the signal to adapt.

## Who it suits

People with a genuinely poor intake of fruit and vegetables. For everyone else,
food easily covers it.

## Practical notes

Very large doses commonly cause stomach upset and can raise the risk of kidney
stones in susceptible people. Food-level amounts carry neither the
adaptation-blunting concern nor the side effects — aim to eat it, not megadose
it.
