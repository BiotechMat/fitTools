## What it is

"Natural testosterone boosters" are blends sold to raise testosterone and, by
extension, muscle and libido. As a category, they are built more on hope than on
evidence.

## What the evidence shows

**Raising testosterone in healthy men.** <EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" basis="human" />
Most over-the-counter blends do not reliably raise testosterone in controlled
studies. Where an ingredient helps, it is usually by correcting a genuine
deficiency — of zinc or vitamin D, for example — not by boosting a normal level.

## Who it suits

Very few people. Genuinely low testosterone is a medical matter to discuss with
a doctor, and evidence-based treatment is very different from an over-the-counter
"booster".

## Practical notes

The proprietary-blend model makes the category risky: undisclosed ingredients,
the occasional banned substance — a real problem for drug-tested athletes — and
rare reports of liver harm. If you suspect low testosterone, get tested rather
than self-medicating. Common ingredients like
[tribulus](/supplements/tribulus-terrestris) and [ZMA](/supplements/zma) don't
deliver on the promise in people who aren't deficient.
