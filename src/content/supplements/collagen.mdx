## What it is

Collagen is the main structural protein in connective tissue — tendons,
ligaments, skin and cartilage. As a supplement it's usually sold as hydrolysed
collagen (collagen peptides), an animal-derived powder.

## What the evidence shows

**Tendons, joints and connective tissue.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
This is the interesting, emerging use: some evidence that collagen (often with
vitamin C, taken shortly before loading the tissue) may support tendon and joint
health and ease some joint pain. Promising and plausible, but still preliminary.

**Skin.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
Some trials report modest improvements in skin hydration and elasticity. The
effects are small and the research is heavily commercial, so keep expectations
measured.

**Building muscle.** <EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" />
Collagen is a **poor muscle-building protein** — it lacks enough leucine and is
an incomplete protein. For muscle, whey or a good mixed-protein diet beats it
easily. Counting collagen toward your protein target for muscle is a mistake.

## Who it suits

People focused on tendon, joint or skin support who understand the evidence is
early — not people looking for a muscle-building protein.

## Practical notes

**Hydrolysed collagen (peptides)** is the studied, absorbable form, commonly
paired with **vitamin C** and taken before loading the target tissue. It is
animal-derived (not vegan). For muscle, reach for
[whey or another quality protein](/supplements/whey-protein) instead.
