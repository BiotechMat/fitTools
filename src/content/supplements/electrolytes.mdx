## What it is

Electrolytes are minerals (chiefly sodium, plus potassium, magnesium and others) that carry the electrical signals behind muscle contraction and fluid balance.
You lose them, mainly sodium, in sweat.

## What the evidence shows

**Replacing heavy sweat losses in prolonged exercise.** <EvidenceTier tier="well-supported" basis="human" />
This is the legitimate, well-established use: during long, hot or very sweaty
sessions, replacing sodium (with fluid) supports performance and helps prevent
the dangerous dilution of blood sodium that plain water alone can cause.

**A daily electrolyte drink for everyone.** <EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" />
The booming "electrolytes for daily wellness" trend is largely marketing. A
normal diet already provides plenty for everyday life; most people don't need to
add more.

**Curing fatigue, brain fog or hangovers.** <EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" />
Not supported as specific, reliable effects.

## Who it suits

Endurance athletes and anyone doing prolonged or hot, sweaty exercise. For a
short gym session or a normal day, water and food usually cover it.

## Practical notes

The key electrolyte to replace is **sodium**, roughly in proportion to how much
you sweat and for how long. Read the safety box. Most people already eat ample
sodium, and those with high blood pressure shouldn't pile on more without reason.
Work out fluid needs with the [water intake calculator](/water-intake-calculator).
