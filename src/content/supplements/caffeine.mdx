## What it is

Caffeine is a stimulant that blocks adenosine, the signal that makes you feel
tired, reducing perceived effort and increasing alertness. It is found in coffee,
tea and, in measured doses, pre-workouts and tablets.

## What the evidence shows

**Endurance and perceived effort.** <EvidenceTier tier="well-supported" basis="human" />
Caffeine is one of the genuinely proven ergogenic aids. The ISSN position stand
concludes it improves endurance performance and reduces how hard a given effort
feels, across many well-controlled trials.

**Strength and power.** <EvidenceTier tier="well-supported" basis="human" />
Benefits here are real but generally smaller and more variable than for
endurance, useful for training quality and strength-endurance more than a
reliable one-rep-max boost.

**Fat burning as a headline claim.** <EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" />
Caffeine slightly raises metabolic rate and is in most fat-burner products, but
this is a minor effect and not a meaningful weight-loss strategy on its own.

## Who it suits

Most people training for endurance or wanting sharper focus and lower perceived
effort. Habitual coffee drinkers still benefit, though the acute kick is blunted.

## Practical notes

Effects build over **30 to 60 minutes** and the half-life is around **5 hours**, so
afternoon and evening doses can wreck sleep, the thing that actually drives
recovery. Effective performance doses are individual and often lower than people
assume. See how long a dose lingers with the
[caffeine calculator](/caffeine-calculator).
