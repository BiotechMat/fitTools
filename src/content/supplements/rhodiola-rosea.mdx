## What it is

Rhodiola rosea is a herb classed as an adaptogen — a substance said to help the
body cope with stress. It works on the stress response rather than acting as a
direct stimulant like caffeine.

## What the evidence shows

**Fatigue, especially mental fatigue.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
Several small trials suggest it may reduce fatigue — particularly the mental,
burnout-type tiredness — and perhaps lower perceived effort during exercise. The
studies are small and mixed, so treat it as promising rather than established.

## Who it suits

People dealing with stress-related fatigue who want to try a gentler,
non-stimulant option. It is not a performance booster in the league of the
well-evidenced ergogenics.

## Practical notes

Studies most often use standardised extracts over a period of weeks, so any
effect builds gradually. Because herbal potency varies widely between brands,
standardisation and product quality matter more here than with a single defined
molecule. It can occasionally be over-stimulating, so avoid taking it late in
the day.
