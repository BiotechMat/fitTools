## What it is

Iron is essential for carrying oxygen in the blood. It is one of the clearest
"test, don't guess" supplements: correcting a genuine deficiency clearly helps,
but supplementing without a shortfall is pointless and carries real risks.

## What the evidence shows

**Correcting deficiency.** <EvidenceTier tier="well-supported" basis="human" />
When a blood test shows you are low, correcting iron deficiency clearly improves
energy and endurance.

**Supplementing when replete.** <EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" />
Taking iron when your levels are already normal offers no performance benefit
and risks harmful build-up.

## Who it suits

People with a tested deficiency. Those most at risk include people who
menstruate, vegetarians and vegans, and endurance athletes, whose turnover and
losses are higher.

## Practical notes

The body has no easy way to excrete excess iron, so over-supplementing can cause
harmful accumulation, and some people carry a genetic tendency to overload —
which is why you should not supplement without testing. Iron tablets are a
leading cause of poisoning in young children, so store them out of reach.
Supplements also commonly cause constipation.
