## What it is

HMB (beta-hydroxy beta-methylbutyrate) is a metabolite of the amino acid
leucine, which you already get from protein. It is marketed for muscle growth
and reduced muscle breakdown.

## What the evidence shows

**Muscle growth in trained lifters.** <EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" basis="human" />
In well-fed, trained people, better-controlled studies have struggled to
reproduce the dramatic early results, and the overall picture is underwhelming.

**Anti-catabolic role in specific situations.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
Its more plausible use is reducing muscle breakdown during heavy dieting,
injury, or in older or untrained people, not adding slabs of muscle to a
healthy trainee.

## Who it suits

There may be a niche during aggressive fat-loss phases or for those returning
from a lay-off, but for most trained lifters eating enough protein it adds
little.

## Practical notes

If your protein intake is adequate, you already produce some HMB, which is one
reason supplementing often does nothing noticeable. Prioritise total protein and
training first, see the [macro calculator](/macro-calculator), and treat HMB
as a low-priority extra rather than a core supplement.
