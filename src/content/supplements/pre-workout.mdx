## What it is

A pre-workout is a mixed-ingredient powder taken before training, promising
energy, focus and "pumps". It's less a single supplement than a bundle — and the
bundle's value depends entirely on what's in it and at what dose.

## What the evidence shows

**The proven ingredients.** <EvidenceTier tier="well-supported" basis="human" />
Most of a good pre-workout's real effect comes from ingredients that work in
their own right: [caffeine](/supplements/caffeine) (the bulk of the kick),
[beta-alanine](/supplements/beta-alanine) for repeated high-intensity efforts,
and [citrulline](/supplements/citrulline-malate) as a modest maybe. The benefit
is those ingredients, not the brand.

**Proprietary blends and the long extra ingredient list.** <EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" />
Many products bury effective ingredients in an underdosed "proprietary blend" and
pad the label with extras that have little or no evidence. A big ingredient list
is not the same as a big effect.

**Focus/nootropic and "explosive" claims.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
Mostly down to the caffeine; the rest is largely unproven at the doses used.

## Who it suits

People who like the convenience and ritual of a pre-workout and choose one with
**transparent, sensible doses** of the proven ingredients — not those chasing the
proprietary-blend hype.

## Practical notes

Read the safety box: most of the punch is **caffeine**, often in high doses, so
mind your total daily intake and timing (our
[caffeine calculator](/caffeine-calculator) helps). You can replicate a good
pre-workout by buying the evidence-based ingredients separately and controlling
the doses.
