## What it is

Ashwagandha (*Withania somnifera*) is a herb used in traditional Ayurvedic
medicine, marketed today as an "adaptogen", something claimed to help the body
resist stress. Standardised root extracts are the common supplement form.

## What the evidence shows

**Stress and cortisol.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
This is the best-studied claim: several small randomised trials report reduced
perceived stress and lower cortisol over about eight weeks. The trials are
**small and often industry-funded**, so treat it as promising rather than
established.

**Sleep.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
Some evidence for modest improvements in sleep quality, again from limited
studies.

**Strength, muscle and testosterone.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
A handful of small studies suggest modest gains in strength or recovery, and some
report small testosterone changes. The base is thin and not convincing on its own, nowhere near creatine's evidence.

**Broad "hormone-optimising" claims.** <EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" />
Sweeping claims about transforming hormones or performance run well ahead of the
data.

## Who it suits

People mainly interested in the stress/sleep angle who understand the evidence is
early, and who have checked the safety points above, which are the real
consideration here.

## Practical notes

Study effects built over roughly **8 weeks** of daily use, so it isn't an acute
aid. As a herbal product, potency and purity vary between brands. If you're
tracking recovery, the [recovery readiness index](/recovery-readiness-index) is
a more direct signal than any supplement.
