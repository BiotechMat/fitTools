## What it is

Glutamine is the most abundant amino acid in the body, widely sold for muscle
growth and immune support. The body makes it, and any protein-containing diet
supplies more.

## What the evidence shows

**Muscle and strength.** <EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" basis="human" />
In healthy people who eat enough protein, controlled studies do not support
meaningful gains in muscle or strength.

**Immunity and the gut.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
Its credible evidence is in specific clinical settings — serious illness, trauma
or gut injury — not in healthy athletes. Extrapolating from those situations to
everyday training is where the marketing overreaches.

## Who it suits

Mainly patients in defined medical contexts under supervision. For a healthy
lifter it is one of the clearest examples of a well-marketed, low-value
supplement.

## Practical notes

Because the body already produces glutamine and gets more from food,
supplementing rarely changes anything for a well-fed trainee. Spend your budget
on total protein and training instead.
