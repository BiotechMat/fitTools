## What it is

CJC-1295 is a synthetic analogue of growth-hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH),
engineered to last far longer in the body than the natural signal. It is heavily
marketed for muscle and anti-ageing, often paired in that marketing with
faster-acting secretagogues.

## Composition

It is a modified GHRH peptide. A version was designed to bind to a blood protein
so it persists much longer than natural GHRH. What is sold online is a
laboratory-made research chemical.

## Proposed mechanism

As a GHRH analogue, it tells the pituitary to release growth hormone, raising
circulating growth hormone and IGF-1, the pathway the muscle and anti-ageing
claims hang on.

## Claimed benefits

Increased muscle, reduced fat, improved recovery and "anti-ageing" effects.

## What the evidence shows

<EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" basis="mixed" />. Early studies established
that it can raise growth hormone and IGF-1, but raising a hormone is not the same
as producing muscle or fat-loss results. There are no robust long-term trials
demonstrating the physique or anti-ageing benefits it is marketed for.

## Legal & regulatory status

Not an approved medicine, sold as a research chemical. GHRH and its analogues are
named on the WADA Prohibited List under peptide hormones and growth factors, and
are banned in sport at all times.

## Known risks & unknowns

Its long-term safety in healthy people is unknown, and research-chemical products
carry purity and contamination risks. Sustained elevation of growth hormone and
IGF-1 has theoretical long-term risks that have not been well characterised for
this compound.
