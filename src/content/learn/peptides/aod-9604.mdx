## What it is

AOD-9604 is a synthetic fragment based on part of the growth-hormone molecule,
originally developed by a pharmaceutical company as a potential anti-obesity
treatment. It is unusual in this space because it was actually tested in humans.

## Composition

It corresponds to a small fragment of the growth-hormone molecule, related in
concept to other "fat-loss" growth-hormone fragments. What is sold online is a
research chemical.

## Proposed mechanism

It was proposed to stimulate fat breakdown without the broader growth-hormone
effects on blood sugar, the rationale for developing it as a weight-loss drug.

## Claimed benefits

Targeted fat loss without the downsides of growth hormone.

## What the evidence shows

<EvidenceTier tier="not-supported" basis="human" />. This is the unusual case
where the compound was properly tested: in human clinical trials for obesity, it
did not produce significantly more weight loss than placebo, and development for
that use did not succeed. The fat-loss claims now made for it are contradicted by
the very trials meant to prove them.

## Legal & regulatory status

It is a non-approved substance. That places it in a prohibited category under
WADA rules, and anti-doping authorities have treated it as banned; athletes
should regard it as prohibited.

## Known risks & unknowns

In the obesity trials it was reasonably tolerated, but "didn't clearly harm" is
not the same as "works", and long-term safety for the way it is used today is not
established. Research-chemical products also carry purity and contamination
risks.
