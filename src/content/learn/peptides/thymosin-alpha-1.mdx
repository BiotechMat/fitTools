## What it is

Thymosin alpha-1 is a naturally occurring peptide involved in regulating the
immune system. As the drug thymalfasin it is approved in several countries for
specific immune-related uses — which sets it apart from most compounds marketed
to gym-goers.

## Composition

It is a synthetic version of a peptide fragment produced by the thymus gland. In
its approved form it is a defined pharmaceutical; it is also sold as a research
chemical.

## Proposed mechanism

It modulates immune-cell activity. That immune role is the basis for its approved
medical uses, and for the broader "wellness" and recovery claims made online.

## Claimed benefits

Better immunity, faster recovery and general "wellness" for healthy people.

## What the evidence shows

<EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" basis="human" />. Its evidence lies in
defined clinical, immune-related settings — such as an adjunct in certain
infections — not in improving training, recovery or general wellness in healthy
people. Extrapolating from its approved uses to a fitness benefit is where the
marketing overreaches; that benefit is not established.

## Legal & regulatory status

Approved in a number of countries for immune-related medical uses, but not for
fitness or anti-ageing. Notably, WADA has clarified that thymosin alpha-1 is not
prohibited — unlike thymosin beta-4 (TB-500), which is banned. Two similarly
named peptides therefore have very different anti-doping status.

## Known risks & unknowns

In its approved uses it has been studied and given under supervision.
Self-directed use of research-chemical versions for unproven goals lacks that
oversight and carries the usual purity and contamination risks.
