## What it is

HGH Fragment 176-191 is a piece of the growth-hormone molecule marketed as a
targeted fat-loss peptide. It was actually developed as a drug candidate
(AOD-9604), which means — unlike most compounds here — it has real human
trial data. That data is the reason it sits in the &ldquo;marketing
claim&rdquo; tier.

## Composition

It corresponds to amino acids 176–191 — the tail end — of the human
growth-hormone molecule. The idea was to isolate the part thought to drive
fat metabolism while leaving out growth hormone&rsquo;s other actions
(including its effects on growth and blood sugar).

## Proposed mechanism

It is proposed to stimulate fat breakdown (lipolysis) and inhibit fat
formation, mimicking growth hormone&rsquo;s effect on fat tissue without
raising IGF-1 or blood glucose.

## Claimed benefits

Targeted fat loss without the side effects of growth hormone — a very heavily
promoted &ldquo;fat-burning peptide&rdquo; pitch.

## What the evidence shows

<EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" basis="human" />. This is the clearest
claimed-vs-shown case in this section. As the drug candidate AOD-9604, the
fragment went through multiple human trials — and its largest Phase IIb
obesity trial **failed to show a significant benefit**, after which
development was discontinued in 2007. The compound was generally tolerated,
but &ldquo;tolerated&rdquo; is not &ldquo;effective.&rdquo; The strong
fat-loss marketing is not supported by the human evidence that actually
exists.

## Legal & regulatory status

Never approved as a weight-loss medicine — development was abandoned. Sold
as a research chemical. As an unapproved growth-hormone-related peptide it
should be treated as prohibited in sport under the WADA code.

## Known risks & unknowns

Trials reported reasonable short-term tolerability, but there is no approved
product, no long-term safety data for this use, and research-chemical
versions carry purity and dosing unknowns. The bigger &ldquo;risk&rdquo; for
most buyers is simpler: paying for an effect the pivotal human trial
didn&rsquo;t deliver.
