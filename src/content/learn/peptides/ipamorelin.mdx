## What it is

Ipamorelin is a synthetic peptide that prompts the body to release a pulse of
its own growth hormone. It is often marketed as a "cleaner" secretagogue because
it has little effect on appetite or the stress hormone cortisol, unlike some
older peptides in its class.

## Composition

It is a short synthetic peptide that mimics ghrelin — the hunger hormone — at
the pituitary, where it triggers growth-hormone release. What is sold online is
a laboratory-made research chemical.

## Proposed mechanism

By acting on the ghrelin receptor, it stimulates the pituitary to secrete a
burst of growth hormone, which the marketing links to muscle gain, fat loss and
"anti-ageing".

## Claimed benefits

More muscle, less fat, better recovery and slowed ageing.

## What the evidence shows

<EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" basis="mixed" />. Raising growth hormone is
real pharmacology, but that does not automatically produce meaningful muscle
gain or fat loss in healthy adults. There are no good long-term human trials
demonstrating the physique or anti-ageing benefits it is sold for, so those
claims are not supported.

## Legal & regulatory status

Not an approved medicine for muscle-building or anti-ageing use, and sold as a
research chemical with no quality control. Growth-hormone secretagogues are
explicitly named on the WADA Prohibited List and are banned in sport at all
times.

## Known risks & unknowns

Its long-term safety in healthy people is not established, and research-chemical
products carry additional purity and contamination risks. Sustained manipulation
of the growth-hormone axis has theoretical long-term risks that have not been
well characterised for this compound.
