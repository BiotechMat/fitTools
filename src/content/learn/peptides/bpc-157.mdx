## What it is

BPC-157 is a synthetic peptide marketed heavily for healing, tendons,
ligaments, gut, muscle. It is one of the most-hyped compounds in this space,
and also one where the gap between the marketing and the human evidence is
widest.

## Composition

It is a synthetic 15-amino-acid peptide. It is described in the research
literature as a &ldquo;stable gastric pentadecapeptide&rdquo; based on a
sequence said to be derived from a protein found in gastric juice. What is
sold online is a laboratory-made synthetic; the &ldquo;body protection
compound&rdquo; name is marketing framing, not a proven physiological role in
humans.

## Proposed mechanism

It is proposed to promote healing by supporting new blood-vessel formation
(angiogenesis) and modulating growth-factor and nitric-oxide pathways. These
mechanisms come largely from laboratory and animal work.

## Claimed benefits

Faster recovery from tendon, ligament, muscle and gut injury, and a general
&ldquo;heals everything&rdquo; reputation online.

## What the evidence shows

<EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="animal" />. There is a substantial
body of positive research, but it is overwhelmingly in rodents, reporting
faster healing of various tissues. There are **no published randomised
controlled trials in humans**. That is the crux: promising animal findings
frequently fail to reproduce in people, and until human trials exist,
whether BPC-157 does anything in humans, at any dose, is genuinely unknown.
Claims of proven human healing are not supported.

## Legal & regulatory status

Not an approved medicine. In 2023 the US FDA moved to restrict its use in
compounded products, citing insufficient safety data. Sold as a research
chemical with no quality control. As an unapproved substance it falls under
WADA&rsquo;s S0 category (no current regulatory approval for human use),
meaning it is prohibited in sport at all times.

## Known risks & unknowns

The honest answer on safety is: unknown. Without human safety trials, there
is no established side-effect profile or long-term data, and research-chemical
products carry additional purity and contamination risks. &ldquo;No reported
problems&rdquo; from anecdotes is not the same as demonstrated safety.
