## What it is

TB-500 is a peptide marketed for recovery and tissue repair, closely tied to
thymosin beta-4, a naturally-occurring repair-related peptide. It is often
paired with BPC-157 in marketing, and shares the same core problem: real lab
biology, no controlled human evidence for the fitness claims.

## Composition

Thymosin beta-4 (Tβ4) is a naturally-occurring 43-amino-acid peptide.
&ldquo;TB-500&rdquo; is the name commonly used for a synthetic version — often
described as an active fragment — sold as a research chemical. The two names
are used loosely and are not always the identical molecule.

## Proposed mechanism

Thymosin beta-4 regulates actin, a protein central to how cells move,
migrate and rebuild tissue. Through that role it is proposed to support
wound healing, blood-vessel formation and cell migration to injured areas.

## Claimed benefits

Faster injury recovery, reduced inflammation, improved flexibility and
tissue repair.

## What the evidence shows

<EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="animal" />. The mechanism is real and
thymosin beta-4 has been studied in humans for specific medical conditions
(for example certain eye-surface and cardiac indications). But the
recovery-and-repair claims for athletes rest on cell and animal studies plus
mechanistic reasoning — there are no controlled human trials showing it
improves athletic recovery. So that use is unproven, not disproven; the
evidence simply isn&rsquo;t there yet.

## Legal & regulatory status

Not an approved medicine for fitness use. Sold as a research chemical. TB-500
/ thymosin beta-4 is prohibited in sport under the WADA code (growth factors
and related peptides) at all times.

## Known risks & unknowns

Long-term safety for fitness use is not established. Because Tβ4 influences
cell migration and blood-vessel growth, there are theoretical concerns around
anything that could promote unwanted tissue growth — concerns that human
safety trials for this use have not addressed. Research-chemical products add
the usual purity and contamination unknowns.
