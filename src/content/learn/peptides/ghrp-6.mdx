## What it is

GHRP-6 is one of the earlier growth-hormone-releasing peptides. A defining
feature is that it strongly stimulates appetite, which sets it apart from the
"cleaner" newer peptides marketed to avoid that effect.

## Composition

It is a synthetic peptide that mimics ghrelin at the pituitary. What is sold
online is a laboratory-made research chemical.

## Proposed mechanism

By acting on the ghrelin receptor, it prompts the pituitary to release growth
hormone, and also drives a strong increase in hunger.

## Claimed benefits

More muscle, improved recovery and, framed as a benefit, a big appetite boost to
support eating for size.

## What the evidence shows

<EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" basis="mixed" />. It reliably raises growth
hormone and appetite, but neither guarantees muscle gain, and there are no solid
long-term human trials showing the physique benefits it is sold for. The strong
appetite stimulation is often reframed in marketing as a feature rather than a
side effect.

## Legal & regulatory status

Not an approved medicine, sold as a research chemical. Growth-hormone-releasing
peptides are explicitly listed on the WADA Prohibited List and banned in sport at
all times.

## Known risks & unknowns

Its long-term safety in healthy people is not established, and as with other
research-chemical peptides, purity and contamination are additional concerns. The
marked appetite stimulation can itself be an unwanted effect.
