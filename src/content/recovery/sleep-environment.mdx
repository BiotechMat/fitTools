## Why your sleep environment matters

Sleep is the single most powerful recovery tool there is, and your bedroom
environment is one of the most controllable inputs to it. The good news: the
factors that matter most are cheap and simple, and the pricey gadgets mostly
aren't essential.

## What the evidence shows

**A cool room.** <EvidenceTier tier="well-supported" basis="human" />
Your body lowers its core temperature to fall and stay asleep, and a room that's
too warm is a common, fixable cause of restless nights. Temperature is one of the
better-supported environmental factors (a slightly cool room, often cited around
16 to 19°C, suits most people).

**Darkness.** <EvidenceTier tier="well-supported" basis="human" />
Darkness supports melatonin and sleep; light, especially bright light in the
hours before bed, works against it. Blackout and reducing evening light are
high-value, low-cost wins.

**Quiet.** <EvidenceTier tier="well-supported" basis="human" />
Noise disrupts sleep even when it doesn't fully wake you. Reducing it (or masking
it consistently) helps.

**Sleep gadgets.** <EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" />
Cooling mattresses, trackers and the like are optional extras. Some may help at
the margins, but they're not the fundamentals.

## How to apply it

Get the basics right first: **cool, dark, quiet**. Fix a too-warm room, block
light, and cut evening bright light before spending on gadgets. If poor sleep,
loud snoring or daytime exhaustion persist, see the safety box. That's a job for
a doctor, not a gadget.
