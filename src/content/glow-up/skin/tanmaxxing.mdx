## Tanmaxxing is a health warning, not a glow-up

"Tanmaxxing", chasing a deep tan through deliberate UV exposure, tanning beds,
or Melanotan tanning injections and nasal sprays, is one of the clearest
examples of the looksmaxxing world getting it exactly backwards. This is the one
place we don't hedge.

### A tan is DNA damage

<EvidenceTier tier="well-supported" basis="human" /> Tanning is your skin
producing pigment *in response to UV damaging its DNA*. There's no version of a
UV tan that isn't also cumulative damage. The idea of a protective "base tan" is
a myth, it's worth roughly a sun protection factor of about 3, while adding to
the very exposure that causes ageing and skin cancer.

### Tanning beds: a Group 1 carcinogen

<EvidenceTier tier="not-supported" basis="human" /> UV-emitting tanning devices
are classified by the WHO's International Agency for Research on Cancer as
**Group 1, carcinogenic to humans**, the same category as tobacco. Starting
before age 30 is associated with a substantially higher melanoma risk. There is
no safe tanning bed, and "controlled" indoor UV is still the carcinogen.

### Melanotan ("the Barbie drug")

<EvidenceTier tier="not-supported" basis="human" /> Melanotan I and II are
**unlicensed**. They're sold on the black market as injections and nasal sprays
with no guarantee of what's in them, and regulators including the UK's MHRA warn
their safety hasn't been established. Dermatologists have documented **melanoma
and rapid changes to moles** in users, which also makes real skin cancer harder
to spot. For the pharmacology, see our [Melanotan II reference
page](/learn/peptides/melanotan-2).

### The honest alternative

If you want the look of colour without the damage, that's what fake tan is for,
topical DHA products colour the skin without UV. And the actual glow-up here is
[sun protection](/glow-up/skin/sunscreen): the highest-evidence thing you can do
for how your skin looks over time.

## The honest summary

Deliberate UV and Melanotan aren't a shortcut, they're the thing dermatologists
spend their careers warning against. Rated **not supported**, with harm
documented. If you take one thing from this section, let it be this one.
