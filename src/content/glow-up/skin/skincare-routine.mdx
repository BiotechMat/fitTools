## Three steps do most of the work

The skincare industry sells complexity because complexity sells more products.
The evidence points the other way: a short, consistent routine covers almost all
the available benefit, and it's easier to actually keep.

### The routine that matters

1. **Cleanse — gently.** A mild cleanser once or twice a day. Harsh, stripping
   washes damage the barrier and make everything else harder.
2. **Moisturise.** Whatever suits your skin — the job is a comfortable, intact
   barrier. Dry, over-stripped skin looks and feels worse, not "cleaner".
3. **Protect.** <EvidenceTier tier="well-supported" basis="human" /> Sunscreen
   every morning. This is the single step with randomised-trial evidence for how
   your skin ages, so it's the one to never skip. See [the sunscreen
   breakdown](/glow-up/skin/sunscreen).

That's it for the essentials. Barrier care plus sun protection is most of skin
health.

### The one worthwhile add-on

<EvidenceTier tier="well-supported" basis="human" /> A **night-time retinoid**,
if your goal is ageing. It has the best evidence after sunscreen — introduced
slowly so it doesn't irritate. [The honest retinoid
guide](/glow-up/skin/retinoids) covers how.

### What you can drop

<EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" /> Toners, essences, stacks of serums,
"10-step" routines. Most are optional at best. More products means more cost,
more irritation risk, and more steps to abandon on a busy week — not better
skin. If you enjoy them, fine; just don't mistake them for the fundamentals.

### The unglamorous multipliers

<EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" /> Sleep and overall health do
real work here — skin repairs overnight, and it's a free lever. Our [sleep
calculator](/sleep-calculator) is a sensible place to start.

## The honest summary

Cleanse, moisturise, protect — plus a retinoid if you want one. A routine you
actually keep beats an elaborate one you quit, every time.
