## The myths that keep people out of sunscreen

A lot of sunscreen "advice" online works against the one skincare habit with
real trial evidence. Here are the common ones, rated.

### "A base tan protects you"

<EvidenceTier tier="not-supported" basis="human" /> A tan is your skin reacting
to UV damage, and a "base tan" offers roughly a sun protection factor of 3 at
best — while adding to your lifetime UV load. It's not protection; it's more of
the exposure that causes the harm.

### "Tanning beds are a safer way to tan"

<EvidenceTier tier="not-supported" basis="human" /> UV-emitting tanning devices
are classified by the WHO's cancer agency as **Group 1 carcinogens** — the same
category as tobacco — and starting young raises melanoma risk substantially.
There is no safe tanning bed. We go deeper in [tanmaxxing and
Melanotan](/glow-up/skin/tanmaxxing).

### "Sunscreen is toxic / worse than the sun"

<EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" /> The viral "sunscreen is dangerous"
claims run far ahead of the evidence. Regulators keep reviewing how much of
certain filters is absorbed, which is normal science — but the large,
well-established risk is UV exposure and skin cancer, not wearing sunscreen.
Don't let scaremongering talk you out of the highest-evidence habit; if a
specific filter worries you, mineral (zinc/titanium) sunscreens exist.

### "You only need it when it's sunny / summer"

<EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" /> UVA passes through cloud and
glass year-round, so a daily morning habit is the simplest reliable approach.
The trial evidence for less ageing came from *daily* use, not occasional.

### "High SPF means you can stay out all day"

<EvidenceTier tier="marketing-claim" /> SPF measures burn protection under
ideal, thick application — not a licence for unlimited exposure. Reapplication,
shade and clothing still matter; no bottle makes UV harmless.

## The honest summary

Almost every popular sunscreen myth points the same way: away from the one habit
with randomised-trial evidence. Wear enough, every morning, and treat "base
tans" and tanning beds as the health risks they are.
