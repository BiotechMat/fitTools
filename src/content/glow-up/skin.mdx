## The glow-up, but peer-reviewed

"Looksmaxxing" is one of the biggest health search categories going — and one
of the worst served. It's full of jaw trainers, tanning injections and
face-rating apps. So here's the honest version. The things that genuinely change
how your skin looks are unglamorous, cheap, and backed by actual trials. We rate
the *evidence*, never a person.

The order below is deliberate: it's roughly the order of how much each thing is
supported by evidence, not how much it's marketed.

### 1. Sun protection — the one with trial evidence

<EvidenceTier tier="well-supported" basis="human" /> This is the single highest-leverage
thing on the list, and it's not close. In a randomised trial, adults using
sunscreen daily showed about 24% less skin ageing over four and a half years
than those using it as they pleased, and regular use has trial evidence for
reducing melanoma too. No serum has anything like this behind it.

The health framing matters here: the reason to protect your skin from UV is that
UV is the main preventable cause of skin cancer. Looking younger for longer is
the side-effect, not the point. [Read the sunscreen breakdown →](/glow-up/skin/sunscreen)

### 2. A retinoid, if you want to target ageing

<EvidenceTier tier="well-supported" basis="human" /> After sun protection,
topical retinoids (tretinoin, retinol) have the best evidence for improving the
look of photo-ageing — fine lines, texture, pigmentation — by prompting collagen
production. Prescription tretinoin has the strongest data; over-the-counter
retinol is gentler and weaker. Start low and slow. [The honest retinoid
guide →](/glow-up/skin/retinoids)

### 3. A short, consistent routine

<EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" /> Cleanse, moisturise, protect —
plus an optional night-time retinoid. That covers the basics that actually
matter. A ten-step shelf mostly adds cost and irritation. [What a sensible
routine looks like →](/glow-up/skin/skincare-routine)

### 4. Sleep

<EvidenceTier tier="preliminary" basis="human" /> Skin does much of its repair
overnight, and poor sleep is linked in small studies to slower barrier recovery
and more visible ageing. It's a genuinely promising, free lever — worth as much
attention as your skincare shelf. Our [sleep calculator](/sleep-calculator) is a
place to start.

### What to ignore

<EvidenceTier tier="not-supported" /> Deliberate tanning and Melanotan
injections ("tanmaxxing"), bone-smashing, mewing-for-jawline, and unregulated
peptides or SARMs. These are either unproven or actively dangerous — we cover
them honestly in the [looksmaxxing myths, rated](/glow-up/looksmaxxing-myths)
hub, rather than pretending there are two sides.

## The honest summary

If you did only two things, they'd be: wear sunscreen every morning, and sleep
properly. Add a retinoid if ageing is your focus. That's most of the available
benefit, for very little money — and none of it involves rating your face or
hitting it with anything.
