## Methodology

Your lift is compared against **Lon Kilgore's Strength Standard Tables**
(2023 edition), which grade squat, bench press and deadlift performance
across five stages (physically active, beginner, intermediate, advanced
and elite) by sex and bodyweight. Kilgore built the tables from
scientific data, published drug-tested classic/raw competition results
and decades of coaching records; they represent *reasonable expectations
at each stage of training progression*, deliberately not population
averages.

This page uses the kilogram tables for the 20 to 29 age band as its anchor.
Between the published bodyweight rows we interpolate linearly, and we
clamp at the table edges rather than extrapolating. Below the
physically-active threshold we report "untrained".

## Interpreting your result

The levels describe training progression, not worth. Moving from beginner
to intermediate typically takes months of consistent training; from
intermediate to advanced, years. The next-level threshold shown under
your classification is the concrete number to chase.

Standards differ across websites because no official standard exists.
Each site derives bands from different data and philosophy. We show our
source, its derivation and its limits openly so the comparison is
meaningful.

## Limitations

- The 20 to 29 anchor band overstates expectations for older lifters,
  Kilgore's full tables (linked in Sources) cover ages 15 to 89 and are the
  better reference well outside that band.
- Standards assume full-range, unequipped lifts; high-bar vs low-bar
  squat or pause vs touch-and-go bench all blur comparisons.
- A classification says nothing about technique quality or injury risk.
  Chase the next threshold with both intact.
