## Methodology

Two routes to lean body mass:

1. **Boer formula** (default): men `LBM = 0.407W + 0.267H − 19.2`; women
   `LBM = 0.252W + 0.473H − 48.3` (weight kg, height cm). Published in
   1984 for normalising physiological measures, it remains the most used
   height-and-weight LBM estimate.
2. **Direct calculation** when you enter a body-fat percentage:
   `LBM = weight × (1 − body fat ÷ 100)` — exactly as accurate as the
   body-fat figure you provide.

## Interpreting your result

Lean body mass bundles everything except fat: muscle, bone, organs, water.
Two practical uses dominate. First, tracking: if weight falls but LBM
holds, you're losing fat rather than muscle — the outcome diets aim for.
Second, feeding into lean-mass-based formulas like Katch–McArdle BMR or
protein targets set per kilogram of lean mass.

Expect the Boer estimate and the direct calculation to disagree by a few
kilograms — Boer assumes an average build for your height and weight,
while the direct route reflects your actual composition.

## Limitations

- Boer knows nothing about your training history; muscular people are
  underestimated and sedentary people overestimated.
- LBM includes water, so glycogen and hydration swings move it day to day
  — compare like-for-like conditions.
- No formula replaces a DEXA scan where precision genuinely matters.
