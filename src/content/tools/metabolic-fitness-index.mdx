## Methodology

The Metabolic Fitness Index is an **open composite index** (v1.0.0). Unlike
the scores buried inside proprietary wearables, everything about it is
published: the inputs, the sub-score mappings, the weights, and the
evidence tier of each input. You, or anyone, can reconstruct the number
exactly.

Each input is mapped to a **0 to 100 sub-score** (higher = more favourable)
using a clamped linear scale between documented anchor points, then combined
as a weighted average:

The anchor points come from recognised references, the international CGM
consensus (time in range ≥70%, %CV < 36%), ADA A1C bands for GMI, the 0.5
waist-to-height boundary, and typical adult-to-athletic resting heart
rates. They are self-tracking anchors, **not diagnostic thresholds**. Full
detail on the house methodology (normalisation, weighting, versioning) is
on the [index methodology page](/learn/index-methodology).

If you leave an input blank, the index renormalises across what you provided
and flags reduced confidence, rather than guessing a value.

## Interpreting your result

Read the **breakdown, not just the headline**. The radar and table show
which markers are pulling your score up or down, that's where the useful
information is. A single number is only a summary; the point of publishing
the sub-scores is so you can see *what to work on*, framed as a modifiable
lever rather than a verdict.

Track your own trend over weeks and months. Because the weights and mappings
are fixed and versioned, a change in your score reflects a real change in
your inputs, not a moving goalpost.

## Limitations

- This is a **transparent self-tracking construct, not a medical test**, and
  it has not been validated against health outcomes.
- The sub-score anchors are reasonable but chosen by us; different anchors
  would give different numbers. They are published so you can judge them.
- CGM-derived inputs inherit CGM's limitations, and for people without
  diabetes the value of optimising glucose metrics is uncertain (see the CGM
  metrics tool).
- Any concern about your metabolic health belongs with a clinician and
  proper testing, not a self-tracking score.
