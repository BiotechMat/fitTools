## Methodology

Two complementary volume measures:

- **Tonnage** = sets × reps × load, summed across exercises, total
  mechanical work in kilograms.
- **Hard sets per muscle**, the count of work sets (taken within ~4 reps
  of failure) assigned to each muscle group, the volume currency used in
  modern hypertrophy research.

## Interpreting your result

For muscle growth, the hard-set count is the number to manage.
Meta-analyses (Schoenfeld et al., 2017) show a dose-response
relationship: growth begins with as few as 4 to 5 weekly sets per muscle and
increases with volume up to a point of diminishing, then negative,
returns, with 10 to 20 weekly hard sets a sensible range for most trained
lifters. Log a full week of training here to compare against that range.

Tonnage suits strength-block comparisons: the same exercises across
weeks, where rising tonnage at stable technique signals progressive
overload. Comparing tonnage across different exercises or rep ranges is
close to meaningless, 10,000 kg of deadlifts and 10,000 kg of lateral
raises are different universes.

## Limitations

- Set counting conventions differ (how to credit compound lifts to
  secondary muscles); whatever convention you choose, keep it consistent.
- Warm-ups don't count, and sets stopped far from failure count less
  than the arithmetic suggests.
- More volume only helps if you recover from it, the research
  dose-response assumes adequate sleep, food and time between sessions.
