## Methodology

This calculator builds your macro targets in three steps, in a deliberate
order:

1. **Protein** is set first, from bodyweight: your chosen intake between
   1.6 and 2.2 g per kilogram per day. The default of 1.8 g/kg sits in the
   middle of the range supported by the largest systematic review on the
   topic (Morton et al., 2018), which found muscle-building benefits
   plateau at around 1.6 g/kg, with a margin above for dieting or heavy
   training.
2. **Fat** is set as a share of your calorie target, between 20% and 35% —
   the acceptable macronutrient distribution range from the Institute of
   Medicine's Dietary Reference Intakes.
3. **Carbohydrate** fills whatever calories remain.

Grams and calories convert using the standard Atwater factors:

`protein 4 kcal/g · carbohydrate 4 kcal/g · fat 9 kcal/g`

Nothing is rounded until display, so the three macros always sum to your
calorie target when the split is feasible.

## Interpreting your result

Treat the output as a planning template rather than a daily contract.
Protein is the number worth hitting most consistently — it drives muscle
retention while dieting and growth while gaining. Fat and carbohydrate can
flex against each other according to preference: neither split has been
shown to meaningfully outperform the other for fat loss when calories and
protein are matched.

If the calculator warns that your split is infeasible, your protein and fat
settings already exceed the calorie target — something has to give, and it
is usually the calorie target that is set too low.

## Limitations

- The Atwater factors are averages across foods; the true energy you absorb
  varies with food choice, fibre and processing by a few percent.
- Protein recommendations here are aimed at people who train. Sedentary
  adults need less (the UK reference nutrient intake is 0.75 g/kg), while
  very lean athletes in a deficit may benefit from the top of the range.
- Calorie targets are only as good as the estimate behind them — pair this
  tool with the TDEE calculator and adjust from real-world results.
