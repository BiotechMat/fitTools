## Methodology

Three lines of arithmetic, nothing more:

`concentration (mg/ml) = vial contents (mg) ÷ diluent added (ml)`

`draw volume (ml) = prescribed dose (mg) ÷ concentration`

`U-100 syringe units = draw volume (ml) × 100`

Doses entered in micrograms are converted to milligrams (÷1000) before
the division. The tool holds no compound database, no preset doses and no
recommendations. Every number comes from you, and should come to you
from a prescriber.

## Interpreting your result

The syringe-units figure is where mistakes are cheapest to catch: U-100
markings measure **volume**, so the same "10 units" contains completely
different drug amounts at different concentrations. Whenever you change
the diluent volume, the concentration, and therefore the draw for the
same dose, changes with it. Re-run the arithmetic after any change, and
cross-check against your prescriber's instructions before drawing.

If the tool reports that your dose needs more volume than the vial holds,
the usual culprit is a units mix-up (mg entered as mcg or vice versa).

## Limitations

- Arithmetic assumes complete, uniform dissolution and no overfill;
  actual vial contents can vary slightly from the label.
- This page cannot verify that your entered values are correct or safe.
  It multiplies and divides exactly what you type.
- Sterile technique, storage and administration are entirely outside this
  tool's scope; your pharmacist is the right source for all three.
