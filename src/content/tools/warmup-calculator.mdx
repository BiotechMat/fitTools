## Methodology

The generator builds the widely used ramp to a first work set:

`empty bar × 10 → 40% × 5 → 60% × 3 → 80% × 1 → work set`

Each percentage is then rounded to a weight your plates can actually
build, using the same exact plate-combination search as our plate
calculator — so a 40% set of 36 kg becomes the loadable 35 kg, never a
fictional number. Warm-ups are always capped at or below the work
weight.

## Interpreting your result

The ramp does three jobs: raises muscle temperature (the ×10 bar set),
grooves the movement pattern (the ×5 and ×3), and primes the nervous
system for load (the ×1) — all while keeping fatigue low enough that
your work sets don't suffer. The reps deliberately fall as weight rises;
adding extra reps at 80% costs performance without adding preparation.

The template suits most people most of the time. Lifters with heavy
absolute loads often add a 90% single, and cold environments or long
gaps between sessions justify an extra early set.

## Limitations

- Percentages assume a barbell lift with meaningful load; bodyweight and
  isolation work needs less ceremony.
- The first heavy lift of the day needs the full ramp — later lifts
  sharing muscles usually need only a feeder set or two.
- General preparation (a few minutes raising your heart rate, joint
  mobility where you're stiff) still belongs before the bar work.
