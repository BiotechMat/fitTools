## Methodology

Three published equations estimate basal metabolic rate here. The default
is **Mifflin-St Jeor** (men `10W + 6.25H − 5A + 5`; women
`10W + 6.25H − 5A − 161`, with weight in kg, height in cm, age in years),
repeatedly found the most reliable general-purpose prediction equation.
**Katch-McArdle** (`370 + 21.6 × fat-free mass`) works from lean mass when
you can supply a measured body-fat percentage, and the **revised
Harris-Benedict** (Roza & Shizgal, 1984) is included for comparison with
older references.

## Interpreting your result

BMR is the energy cost of simply existing (organ function, cell turnover,
temperature regulation) measured at complete rest. It is not the number
to eat to: real days include movement, digestion and exercise, which is
what the TDEE calculator adds on top. Prediction equations typically land
within about 10% of laboratory measurement for healthy adults; muscular
people run higher than predicted and very sedentary people lower.

## Limitations

- All three equations describe population averages; individual metabolisms
  legitimately vary around them.
- Katch-McArdle inherits every error in your body-fat estimate.
- BMR falls as you diet (adaptive thermogenesis), so recalculate after
  meaningful weight change.
- Suspected thyroid or metabolic issues are a matter for your GP, not a
  calculator.
