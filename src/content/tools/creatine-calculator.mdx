## Methodology

Doses follow the International Society of Sports Nutrition position stand
(Kreider et al., 2017), the reference document on creatine in sport:

- **Loading (optional)**: `0.3 g per kg bodyweight per day`, typically
  split into four doses, for 5–7 days.
- **Maintenance**: `3–5 g per day`, indefinitely.

Loading saturates muscle creatine stores in about a week; skipping it and
taking 3–5 g daily reaches the same saturation in three to four weeks.
The end state — and the performance effect — is identical.

## Interpreting your result

Creatine monohydrate is the most-studied performance supplement in
existence: consistent small-to-moderate benefits for high-intensity,
repeated-effort work and for lean-mass gain alongside resistance
training. Expect a kilogram or so of quick water-weight gain inside
muscle as stores saturate — that is the mechanism working, not fat gain.

Daily consistency matters far more than timing. If you experience gut
discomfort during loading, split doses further or simply start at the
maintenance dose.

## Limitations

- Guidance applies to healthy adults; anyone with kidney disease, on
  relevant medication, pregnant or under 18 should involve a doctor
  before supplementing.
- Roughly 20–30% of people are "non-responders" whose stores are already
  near-saturated from diet.
- These are published sports-nutrition guidelines, not personal medical
  advice — the calculator does arithmetic on a public formula.
