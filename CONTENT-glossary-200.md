# 200 Fitness, Wellness & Human Biology Terms

A glossary of 200 common terms across training, nutrition, physiology, biochemistry, hormones and health markers, each with a concise plain-English definition. Grouped by theme for scanning; can be re-sorted alphabetically for a site glossary index.

## Training & strength (1–30)

1. **1RM (one-rep max)** — The most weight you can lift for a single repetition of an exercise.
2. **Hypertrophy** — Growth in muscle size from training and adequate protein.
3. **Progressive overload** — Gradually increasing demand (load, reps, volume) to keep adapting.
4. **Training volume** — Total work done, usually sets × reps × load, or weekly hard sets.
5. **Training intensity** — How heavy a load is, typically expressed as a percentage of 1RM.
6. **Tempo** — The speed of each rep phase, often written as four digits (e.g. 3-1-1-0).
7. **Time under tension** — Total duration a muscle is loaded during a set.
8. **Repetition (rep)** — One complete movement of an exercise.
9. **Set** — A group of consecutive reps performed without rest.
10. **Superset** — Two exercises performed back-to-back with no rest between.
11. **Drop set** — Continuing a set at a lighter load after reaching failure.
12. **Rest-pause** — Brief pauses within a set to squeeze out extra reps.
13. **RPE (rate of perceived exertion)** — A subjective 1–10 scale of how hard an effort felt.
14. **RIR (reps in reserve)** — How many more reps you could have done before failure.
15. **Periodization** — Structured variation of training variables over time.
16. **Deload** — A planned lighter week to aid recovery and manage fatigue.
17. **Concentric** — The lifting (muscle-shortening) phase of a rep.
18. **Eccentric** — The lowering (muscle-lengthening) phase of a rep.
19. **Isometric** — A contraction with no change in muscle length (a hold).
20. **Compound exercise** — A movement working multiple joints and muscle groups (e.g. squat).
21. **Isolation exercise** — A single-joint movement targeting one muscle (e.g. bicep curl).
22. **Range of motion** — The full distance a joint moves during an exercise.
23. **Muscular endurance** — The ability to sustain repeated contractions over time.
24. **Strength** — The maximum force a muscle or group can produce.
25. **Power** — Force produced quickly; work divided by time.
26. **Rate of force development** — How fast force is generated, key to explosiveness.
27. **Mechanical tension** — Force on the muscle, the primary driver of hypertrophy.
28. **Metabolic stress** — The "burn" from metabolite build-up during training.
29. **Muscle damage** — Micro-tears from training that prompt repair and adaptation.
30. **Mind-muscle connection** — Consciously focusing on the target muscle during a lift.

## Cardio & endurance (31–50)

31. **VO2 max** — The maximum rate of oxygen your body can use; a key aerobic-fitness marker.
32. **Lactate threshold** — The intensity at which lactate accumulates faster than it clears.
33. **Anaerobic threshold** — The point where energy production shifts strongly to anaerobic pathways.
34. **Aerobic capacity** — The body's ability to produce energy using oxygen.
35. **Zone 2 training** — Easy, conversational-pace cardio that builds aerobic base and mitochondria.
36. **Heart rate reserve** — The gap between resting and maximum heart rate, used to set zones.
37. **Maximum heart rate** — The highest heart rate you can reach, estimated by age formulas.
38. **Resting heart rate** — Heart rate at complete rest; lower generally reflects better fitness.
39. **Heart rate variability (HRV)** — Beat-to-beat variation, used as a recovery and stress marker.
40. **Steady-state cardio** — Continuous exercise at a constant moderate intensity.
41. **HIIT** — High-intensity interval training: hard bursts with recovery periods.
42. **LISS** — Low-intensity steady-state cardio.
43. **Fartlek** — "Speed play"; unstructured mixing of fast and slow running.
44. **Tempo run** — Sustained effort near lactate threshold to raise it.
45. **Interval training** — Alternating work and rest bouts at set intensities.
46. **Running economy** — How much energy you use to run at a given pace.
47. **Cadence** — Steps (or pedal strokes) per minute.
48. **EPOC (afterburn)** — Excess post-exercise oxygen consumption; calories burned recovering.
49. **Cardiac output** — Blood pumped by the heart per minute (rate × stroke volume).
50. **Stroke volume** — Blood ejected by the heart per beat.

## Nutrition & diet (51–85)

51. **Calorie** — A unit of food energy (kilocalorie in everyday use).
52. **Macronutrient** — Energy-providing nutrients: protein, carbohydrate and fat.
53. **Micronutrient** — Vitamins and minerals needed in small amounts.
54. **Protein** — Macronutrient of amino acids for tissue repair and enzymes.
55. **Carbohydrate** — Macronutrient and primary fuel, stored as glycogen.
56. **Dietary fat** — Energy-dense macronutrient essential for hormones and absorption.
57. **Fiber** — Indigestible carbohydrate supporting gut health and satiety.
58. **TDEE (total daily energy expenditure)** — All calories burned in a day.
59. **BMR (basal metabolic rate)** — Energy used at complete rest for basic functions.
60. **RMR (resting metabolic rate)** — Energy at rest; slightly higher than BMR.
61. **NEAT** — Non-exercise activity thermogenesis: calories from daily movement.
62. **TEF (thermic effect of food)** — Calories burned digesting and processing food.
63. **Caloric deficit** — Eating fewer calories than you burn, driving fat loss.
64. **Caloric surplus** — Eating more than you burn, enabling weight/muscle gain.
65. **Maintenance calories** — The intake that keeps weight stable.
66. **Energy balance** — The relationship between calories in and calories out.
67. **Glycemic index** — How quickly a food raises blood glucose relative to a reference.
68. **Glycemic load** — Glycemic index adjusted for actual carbohydrate portion.
69. **Net carbs** — Total carbs minus fiber (and sometimes sugar alcohols).
70. **Complete protein** — A protein containing all nine essential amino acids.
71. **Essential amino acid** — An amino acid the body cannot make and must eat.
72. **Leucine threshold** — The leucine dose thought to maximally trigger muscle synthesis.
73. **Nutrient timing** — Strategically timing intake around training.
74. **Carb cycling** — Alternating higher- and lower-carbohydrate days.
75. **Refeed** — A planned higher-calorie (usually carb) day during a diet.
76. **Reverse dieting** — Gradually raising calories after a diet to limit fat regain.
77. **Intermittent fasting** — Cycling between eating and fasting windows.
78. **Ketosis** — A metabolic state burning fat-derived ketones for fuel.
79. **Ketogenic diet** — A very-low-carb, high-fat diet that induces ketosis.
80. **Glycogen** — Stored carbohydrate in muscle and liver.
81. **Gluconeogenesis** — Making glucose from non-carbohydrate sources.
82. **Satiety** — The feeling of fullness that ends eating.
83. **Bioavailability** — The proportion of a nutrient actually absorbed and used.
84. **Nutrient density** — Nutrients provided per calorie of food.
85. **Protein leverage** — The drive to keep eating until protein needs are met.

## Body composition (86–105)

86. **Body composition** — The proportions of fat, muscle, bone and water in the body.
87. **Body fat percentage** — The share of total body mass that is fat.
88. **Lean body mass** — Total mass minus fat mass.
89. **Fat-free mass** — Everything in the body except fat.
90. **FFMI (fat-free mass index)** — A muscularity metric adjusting lean mass for height.
91. **BMI (body mass index)** — Weight over height squared; a rough population screen.
92. **Visceral fat** — Fat around internal organs, linked to metabolic risk.
93. **Subcutaneous fat** — Fat stored under the skin.
94. **Waist-to-hip ratio** — Waist divided by hip circumference; a fat-distribution marker.
95. **Waist-to-height ratio** — Waist divided by height; a simple central-fat risk marker.
96. **Body recomposition** — Losing fat and gaining muscle simultaneously.
97. **Bulking** — Eating in a surplus to prioritise muscle gain.
98. **Cutting** — Eating in a deficit to lose fat while keeping muscle.
99. **Muscle protein synthesis** — The building of new muscle protein.
100. **Muscle protein breakdown** — The breakdown of muscle protein, balanced against synthesis.
101. **Net protein balance** — Synthesis minus breakdown; positive means muscle gain.
102. **Catabolism** — Metabolic breakdown of tissue for energy.
103. **Anabolism** — Metabolic building of tissue.
104. **Sarcopenia** — Age-related loss of muscle mass and strength.
105. **Hyperplasia** — An increase in the number of muscle cells (vs enlarging existing ones).

## Physiology & anatomy (106–135)

106. **Skeletal muscle** — Voluntary muscle attached to bone that moves the body.
107. **Smooth muscle** — Involuntary muscle in organs and blood vessels.
108. **Cardiac muscle** — The involuntary muscle of the heart.
109. **Type I muscle fiber** — Slow-twitch, fatigue-resistant, endurance-oriented.
110. **Type II muscle fiber** — Fast-twitch, powerful, quicker to fatigue.
111. **Motor unit** — A motor neuron and all the muscle fibers it controls.
112. **Neuromuscular junction** — The synapse where nerve meets muscle.
113. **Sarcomere** — The contractile unit of muscle.
114. **Actin** — The thin filament in muscle contraction.
115. **Myosin** — The thick filament that pulls on actin to contract muscle.
116. **Mitochondria** — Cellular "powerhouses" that produce ATP.
117. **Capillary density** — The number of small blood vessels supplying a tissue.
118. **Tendon** — Connective tissue attaching muscle to bone.
119. **Ligament** — Connective tissue attaching bone to bone.
120. **Fascia** — Connective tissue that wraps and separates muscles and organs.
121. **Cartilage** — Flexible connective tissue cushioning joints.
122. **Synovial fluid** — Lubricating fluid inside joints.
123. **Bone density** — The mineral content of bone; a fracture-risk marker.
124. **Osteoblast** — A cell that builds new bone.
125. **Osteoclast** — A cell that breaks down bone.
126. **Central nervous system** — The brain and spinal cord.
127. **Autonomic nervous system** — Controls involuntary functions like heart rate.
128. **Sympathetic nervous system** — The "fight-or-flight" branch.
129. **Parasympathetic nervous system** — The "rest-and-digest" branch.
130. **Proprioception** — The body's sense of its own position and movement.
131. **Homeostasis** — The maintenance of stable internal conditions.
132. **Blood pressure** — The force of blood against artery walls.
133. **Systolic / diastolic** — Pressure during heartbeats / between heartbeats.
134. **Hemoglobin** — The oxygen-carrying protein in red blood cells.
135. **Hematocrit** — The proportion of blood volume made up of red cells.

## Biochemistry & cellular (136–160)

136. **ATP (adenosine triphosphate)** — The body's immediate energy currency.
137. **Creatine phosphate** — A rapid backup for regenerating ATP during short efforts.
138. **Glycolysis** — Breaking glucose down for energy, with or without oxygen.
139. **Krebs cycle** — The aerobic energy cycle in mitochondria (citric-acid cycle).
140. **Oxidative phosphorylation** — The main oxygen-using ATP-production process.
141. **Aerobic metabolism** — Energy production using oxygen.
142. **Anaerobic metabolism** — Energy production without oxygen.
143. **Lactate** — A by-product of anaerobic glucose breakdown, also a fuel.
144. **Enzyme** — A protein that speeds up biochemical reactions.
145. **Hormone** — A chemical messenger released into the blood.
146. **Neurotransmitter** — A chemical messenger between nerve cells.
147. **Free radical** — A reactive molecule that can damage cells.
148. **Oxidative stress** — Imbalance between free radicals and antioxidants.
149. **Antioxidant** — A compound that neutralises free radicals.
150. **Inflammation** — The immune response to injury or stress; acute or chronic.
151. **Cytokine** — A signalling protein of the immune system.
152. **Autophagy** — The cell's recycling of damaged components.
153. **Apoptosis** — Programmed, orderly cell death.
154. **Protein synthesis** — The cellular building of proteins from amino acids.
155. **mTOR** — A signalling pathway that drives growth and protein synthesis.
156. **AMPK** — An energy-sensing pathway activated by exercise and fasting.
157. **Insulin signaling** — How insulin tells cells to take up glucose.
158. **GLUT4** — The muscle glucose transporter recruited by insulin and exercise.
159. **NAD+** — A coenzyme central to energy metabolism and repair.
160. **Telomere** — Protective DNA caps on chromosomes that shorten with age.

## Hormones & endocrine (161–180)

161. **Testosterone** — Anabolic sex hormone influencing muscle, bone, mood and libido.
162. **Estrogen** — Sex hormone important for reproduction, bone and cardiovascular health.
163. **Cortisol** — The primary stress hormone, following a daily rhythm.
164. **Insulin** — Hormone that lowers blood glucose and drives storage.
165. **Glucagon** — Hormone that raises blood glucose between meals.
166. **Growth hormone** — Hormone supporting growth, repair and fat metabolism.
167. **IGF-1** — A growth factor mediating many effects of growth hormone.
168. **Thyroid hormone (T3/T4)** — Hormones that set metabolic rate.
169. **TSH** — Thyroid-stimulating hormone; the first-line thyroid test.
170. **Leptin** — The satiety hormone signalling energy stores to the brain.
171. **Ghrelin** — The "hunger hormone" that stimulates appetite.
172. **Adrenaline (epinephrine)** — Fight-or-flight hormone raising heart rate and fuel.
173. **Noradrenaline (norepinephrine)** — Stress hormone and neurotransmitter for arousal.
174. **Melatonin** — Hormone that signals darkness and regulates sleep timing.
175. **DHEA** — An adrenal hormone precursor to sex hormones.
176. **Adiponectin** — A fat-cell hormone improving insulin sensitivity.
177. **Insulin sensitivity** — How responsive cells are to insulin (higher is better).
178. **Insulin resistance** — Reduced response to insulin, a metabolic-risk state.
179. **Anabolic hormone** — A hormone promoting tissue building (e.g. testosterone, insulin).
180. **Catabolic hormone** — A hormone promoting tissue breakdown (e.g. cortisol).

## Cardiometabolic & health markers (181–195)

181. **LDL cholesterol** — "Bad" cholesterol associated with arterial plaque.
182. **HDL cholesterol** — "Good" cholesterol that helps clear cholesterol.
183. **Triglycerides** — The main circulating blood fat.
184. **ApoB** — A protein counting atherogenic particles; a precise cardiovascular-risk marker.
185. **Lp(a)** — A largely genetic lipoprotein raising heart-attack and stroke risk.
186. **HbA1c** — Average blood glucose over ~3 months; the standard glucose-control marker.
187. **Fasting glucose** — Blood sugar after an overnight fast.
188. **hs-CRP** — High-sensitivity C-reactive protein, a chronic-inflammation marker.
189. **Lipid panel** — A blood test of cholesterol and triglyceride levels.
190. **Metabolic syndrome** — A cluster of risk factors (waist, blood pressure, glucose, lipids).
191. **Atherosclerosis** — The build-up of plaque narrowing and hardening arteries.
192. **eGFR** — Estimated glomerular filtration rate; a kidney-function marker.
193. **Homocysteine** — An amino acid linked to cardiovascular risk when elevated.
194. **Biological age** — An estimate of physiological age from biomarkers, vs calendar age.
195. **Metabolic flexibility** — The ability to switch efficiently between fat and carb fuel.

## Recovery, sleep & wellness (196–200)

196. **Sleep cycle** — A ~90-minute progression through sleep stages, repeated nightly.
197. **REM sleep** — Rapid-eye-movement sleep, important for memory and mood.
198. **Slow-wave sleep** — Deep sleep central to physical recovery and hormone release.
199. **Circadian rhythm** — The body's ~24-hour internal clock governing sleep and metabolism.
200. **Overtraining syndrome** — Performance decline from training that outpaces recovery.
