## 1. Executive summary

We are building a web-based health, fitness and longevity platform: a large,
SEO-optimised library of best-in-class calculators and tools, each its own landing
page, layered with original longevity metrics and a sticky, shareable engagement
experience. The wedge is free tools that rank in search; the destination is a
returning-user health platform.

**The opportunity.** Consumer demand for health, longevity and self-quantification
is at an all-time high, yet the tooling is either shallow (basic calculator farms
with no depth) or locked inside proprietary black-box apps (WHOOP, Oura,
InsideTracker). Three research-backed shifts — biological-age testing going
mainstream, over-the-counter glucose and wearable data outrunning interpretation,
and the March 2026 cardiovascular-risk guideline overhaul — are creating large
pools of high-intent search traffic with almost no good free tooling behind them.

**The product edge.** Rigorous, cited, evidence-based tools (a credibility moat in
a domain Google treats as "Your Money or Your Life"), plus a genuinely novel
position: **transparent, openly-published, versioned scoring** where every
competitor hides their formulas. Openness is simultaneously the trust signal, the
SEO asset, and the link-building engine.

**The model.** Audience first, monetisation second. Grow traffic and returning
users through SEO and positive-psychology viral loops, then switch on the
pre-built layers: display ads → contextual affiliates → premium subscription →
(longer term) B2B/licensing. Near-zero cash cost to operate; the primary
investment is founder time over a 6–12 month SEO ramp.

**Founder fit.** Built by a founder combining full-stack engineering, an MSc in
Biotechnology (the domain credibility this space demands), and enterprise B2B
commercial/presales experience — a rare three-way fit for a technical health
product that must also rank and monetise.

## 2. Vision & mission

**Vision:** to become the most trusted open reference and toolkit for
understanding and improving personal healthspan.

**Mission:** give people accurate, transparent, evidence-based tools to measure
where their health stands and track it improving over time — without the black

## 3. The opportunity — problem & why now

**The problem.** People increasingly have health numbers (from wearables, OTC
sensors, bloodwork) and strong motivation to act, but nowhere neutral and credible
to interpret them. Existing options split into: (a) thin calculator sites that
answer one query and offer no depth or reason to return; (b) proprietary apps that
lock insight behind subscriptions and undisclosed algorithms; (c) fragmented,
often low-quality content.

**Why now (demand tailwinds, all research-backed):**
- **Wellness is a top-tier consumer priority.** 84% of consumers now rank wellness
  a top priority, inside a global wellness market measured in the trillions.
- **Longevity has gone mainstream.** Demand has shifted from pampering to
  diagnostic-led, personalised health, with up to 60% across markets calling
  healthy ageing a top priority. Biological-age testing alone is a market growing
  from ~$1.8bn (2025) toward ~$4.3bn (2034).
- **Data is outrunning interpretation.** Wearables are the #1 fitness trend for
  2026 (ACSM); OTC continuous glucose monitors (Stelo, Lingo) have opened glucose
  tracking to non-diabetics — millions of new data streams with nowhere to go.
- **A cardiovascular-risk reset.** The March 2026 ACC/AHA guidelines introduced
  ApoB, Lp(a) and a 30-year risk model, yet under 1% of insured adults get ApoB
  tested — a large awareness gap with minimal free tooling.
- **GLP-1 reshaping.** Roughly one in five US households now includes a GLP-1
  user, driving demand for muscle-preservation and nutrition tooling.

Each of these is a concrete, near-term content-and-tool gap, not a vague trend.

## 4. Market analysis

**Reference markets (demand signals).** The closest analogue, the global fitness
app market, is ~$12–14bn in 2026 and projected to roughly triple to $33–45bn by
2033–35 (~13–14% CAGR), with North America the largest share (~35–40%) and the US
the single biggest market. Adjacent and overlapping: the longevity/biological-age
testing market (~$1.8bn→$4.3bn) and the broad health-and-wellness market (measured
in trillions). These size the *demand*, not our direct revenue — our economics are
a web property's (traffic → ads/affiliates/subscription), not an app-store
subscription business.

**TAM / SAM / SOM (framed as monetisable attention):**
- **TAM:** global online search and attention around health, fitness, nutrition
  and longevity tools — hundreds of millions of monthly searches; the associated
  ad + affiliate + subscription value is the addressable pool.
- **SAM:** English-language, US/UK-weighted health-tool and longevity search
  traffic we can realistically target — high-value because US traffic drives the
  overwhelming majority of display-ad revenue.
- **SOM (3-year realistic):** a mid-five- to low-six-figure monthly-session
  audience captured via long-tail SEO and viral loops, monetised across ads,
  affiliates and a small premium base.

**Target segments:**
1. **Optimisers / longevity-minded** (core) — track biomarkers, read Attia/Huberman,
   buy CGMs and test kits. High intent, high affiliate value, high shareability.
2. **Strength & hybrid athletes** — the site's credibility zone; underserved by
   quality free tools; low competition to rank.
3. **GLP-1 & weight-management users** — fast-growing, need muscle-preservation and
   nutrition tooling, strong protein-affiliate fit.
4. **General health searchers** — the broad top-of-funnel (BMI, TDEE, calories) that
   drives volume and feeds the loop.

**Geography:** build for a global audience but optimise the **US experience first**
(imperial-first UX, US-relevant content), because US traffic share is the single
biggest lever on ad and affiliate revenue.

## 5. Product & differentiation

**Three layers, built in sequence.**

**Layer 1 — the tool library (the wedge).** ~30 rigorously-built calculators
across nutrition, strength and recovery, each a hybrid page: interactive tool above
the fold, then genuine editorial depth (methodology, cited formulas, limitations,
FAQ). This depth is what ranks in a YMYL domain and what lifts dwell time and ad
viewability versus bare calculators. (Full spec: `SPEC.md`.)

**Layer 2 — open longevity metrics (the differentiator).** Faithful
implementations of published-but-underused formulas (blood-based biological age,
fitness age, heart age, CGM metrics) plus **original, transparent composite
indices** — openly published, versioned, with a changelog. Every incumbent hides
these; we publish them in full. This is the ownable IP and the E-E-A-T and
link-building asset. (Full spec: `METHODOLOGY.md`.)

**Layer 3 — the sticky engagement platform (the retention & growth engine).**
Progress tracking (self-vs-past-self), flattering "top X%" shareable achievement
cards, an identity quiz, behaviour streaks, tightly-segmented leaderboards, and a
weekly longevity-trends feed — all built on positive psychology, never insecurity.
(Full spec and evidence base: `ROADMAP.md`.)

**Content commerce (a cross-cutting layer).** An evidence-based recovery & wellness
content section (cold immersion, sauna, and similar) targets high-commercial-intent
research traffic — pre-purchase queries that monetise strongly via affiliate and
our own products, while the honest, evidence-tiered house style reinforces rather
than dilutes the credibility moat. (Full spec: `CONTENT.md`.)

**The moat.** Not the calculators (anyone can clone those), but the *combination*:
credibility + open methodology + accumulated longitudinal user data + brand. Each
expansion compounds those; that combination is what a better-funded competitor
cannot simply copy.

## 6. Competitive landscape

- **Calculator farms** (Calculator.net, Omni Calculator): huge scale on head terms,
  but shallow, generic, no depth, no retention, no credibility signal. We beat them
  on depth, trust, and the long/mid-tail; we don't fight them on head terms early.
- **Proprietary tracking apps** (WHOOP, Oura, InsideTracker, Levels): strong data
  and brand, but closed algorithms, hardware/subscription gates, and no free
  utility. We differentiate on openness, breadth of free tools, and web-first
  reach.
- **Longevity clinics & DTC testing** (TruDiagnostic, Function): high-value but
  expensive and narrow. We are the free, accessible interpretation and tracking
  layer that sits upstream of them (and can refer to them via affiliates).
- **Content sites / influencers:** authority but no tooling. We pair credible
  content *with* interactive tools and tracking.

**Positioning statement:** the open, evidence-based alternative — the credible
free toolkit and tracker for people serious about healthspan, without the black
boxes or the paywalls on the basics.

## 7. Business model & revenue streams

Audience first; monetisation switched on in layers as traffic arrives. All
infrastructure is pre-built behind flags so activation is a switch, not a rebuild.

1. **Display advertising** (primary early revenue). Enter via Journey by Mediavine
   at ~1,000 monthly sessions (70% rev share), graduate to premium networks
   (Raptive at 25,000 monthly pageviews, or Mediavine's main network at $5,000
   trailing-12-month ad revenue). Health/wellness commands mid-range display RPMs;
   US traffic share is the key multiplier.
2. **Affiliate** (from launch, contextual). Protein/supplements beside nutrition
   tools; CGMs, wearables and test kits beside longevity tools; equipment beside
   strength tools; and — a major surface — high-intent recovery/wellness content
   (saunas, cold plunges, devices) where readers research before a significant
   purchase (`CONTENT.md`). High-value in this niche (test kits and devices pay
   well); direct brand programmes out-earn generic marketplaces.
3. **Own / branded products** (opportunistic, higher margin). Selling our own or
   branded physical products (e.g. recovery gear, supplements) or digital products
   on commercial content pages. Managed to avoid conflict: the evidence assessment
   stays honest and neutral pages stay separate from commercial ones, so
   credibility is preserved (`CONTENT.md` §1–4).
4. **Premium subscription** (from Phase E6). Free tools stay free forever; monetise
   *persistence* — saved profiles, longitudinal tracking, advanced cards/reports,
   ad-free. Recurring revenue that smooths ad volatility.
5. **B2B / licensing** (longer-term optionality). Embeddable widgets and a scoring
   API for coaches, gyms and other sites; the consumer property becomes
   distribution and the tooling becomes infrastructure. Steadier, higher-margin
   revenue independent of own-traffic.
6. **Data & authority products** (longer-term, handled carefully). Anonymised,
   consented benchmarking and an annual "state of healthspan" report — a media/SEO
   asset and potential B2B/research value.

**Why this order:** an engaged, returning, proud audience monetises at far better
ad RPMs, affiliate conversion and subscription uptake than cold one-off traffic —
so deferring monetisation until the loops form is a revenue-maximising choice, not
a delay.

## 8. Go-to-market & growth strategy

**Engine 1 — SEO (the durable acquisition base).** Win the mid- and long-tail
first (e.g. "plate loading calculator kg", "DOTS calculator", "caffeine half-life",
"ApoB target") where we can rank, not the head terms owned by incumbents. Topical
hub structure, schema markup, one strong page per intent (no thin variants), and a
supporting content cluster per hub build authority, alongside dedicated
commercial-intent content clusters (recovery/wellness — `CONTENT.md`) that both
rank and monetise. Move fast on the cardiovascular cluster specifically — the March
2026 guideline change created a live traffic spike with minimal competition.

**Engine 2 — viral loops (the growth accelerator).** Positive-psychology sharing:
flattering "top X%" achievement cards fired at the moment of peak satisfaction,
an identity quiz, and shareable progress milestones — the Spotify-Wrapped / Wordle
mechanic applied to health. Grounded in retention and social-comparison research
(see `ROADMAP.md`).

**Engine 3 — authority & PR.** Open, versioned methodology and an annual data
report are natural backlink and press magnets; embeddable widgets seed inbound
links. Author credibility (biotech MSc, cited sources) underpins ranking and trust.

**Retention** (the multiplier on both engines): longitudinal progress tracking,
graceful streaks, weekly trend content, and a newsletter turn one-off visitors into
a returning, owned audience.

## 9. Operations & technology

- **Stack:** statically-generated web app (Next.js/Astro), all calculation
  client-side, hosted on Vercel/Cloudflare. New tools are config + formula +
  content files against a shared component library — near-zero marginal
  engineering per tool. (Details: `SPEC.md`.)
- **Cost structure (lean, bootstrapped, UK sole trader):** hosting scales from
  ~$20 to low-hundreds/month with traffic; domain, email, consent platform and
  analytics are modest; near-zero fixed cash cost. The real input is **founder
  time**. Optional later spend: freelance content/design, premium-tier infra.
- **Compliance/operations:** medical disclaimers, a consent platform, affiliate
  disclosures, and HMRC accounting for income. Data-protection obligations step up
  when the product moves from stateless tools to stored health data — deliberately
  sequenced as a considered threshold (see Risks), not a launch blocker.

## 11. Financial plan (illustrative)

**Cost:** effectively bootstrapped. Cash costs are hosting + tooling (tens to low
hundreds of dollars/month), so the venture is close to cash-flow neutral from day
one; the investment is founder time over the SEO ramp.

**Revenue — illustrative scenarios.** New domains typically take 6–12 months to
build meaningful organic traffic; these are scenario markers, **contingent on
execution and SEO ramp**, not forecasts. Assumptions: blended session RPM rising
from ~$8 (early, Journey, low US share) toward ~$18–25 (mature, premium network,
better US share); affiliate revenue from high-intent product links (device/test-kit
commissions are meaningful in this niche); a small premium base converting at ~£5/mo.

| Stage (approx.) | Monthly sessions | Ads | Affiliate | Premium | Blended (USD/mo) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ramp (~Mo 6) | ~5,000 | ~$40 | minimal | — | pre-revenue / validation |
| Growth (~Mo 12) | ~30,000 | ~$300–450 | ~$200–400 | — | ~$500–850 |
| Scale (~Mo 18) | ~100,000 | ~$1,500–2,500 | ~$1,000–2,000 | early | ~$3,000–5,000 |

**Path to profitability:** because fixed costs are negligible, the site is

this from a traffic-dependent utility into a durable, diversified asset.

**Strategic (non-cash) return:** even in a conservative traffic scenario, the
project is a high-value portfolio and credibility asset — a shipped, technically
sophisticated, domain-credible health-tech product — directly relevant to the
founder's target commercial roles in life-sciences software and health tech.

## 13. Risks & mitigations

- **SEO ramp uncertainty (primary risk).** Organic traffic is slow and not
  guaranteed. *Mitigation:* near-zero cost means a long runway; viral loops provide
  a non-SEO acquisition channel; target the low-competition mid/long-tail and the
  time-sensitive cardiovascular window first.
- **Ad-revenue volatility & dependence.** RPMs swing and tool pages dwell less than
  content. *Mitigation:* diversify early into affiliates, then premium and B2B;
  never rely on ads alone.
- **Competition / cloneability.** Tools are copyable. *Mitigation:* compound the
  non-copyable moat — credibility, open methodology, longitudinal data, brand.
- **YMYL / accuracy risk.** Wrong health outputs are both harmful and
  reputation-destroying. *Mitigation:* the `METHODOLOGY.md` discipline —
  primary-source coefficients, reproduced test vectors, disclaimers, no medical
  advice, no mortality outputs.
- **Commercial conflict eroding credibility.** Selling own products / affiliates on
  the same site that objectively assesses evidence risks undermining the trust that
  is the core moat. *Mitigation:* evidence-tiered honesty even where inconvenient,
  neutral pages kept separate from commercial ones, clear disclosure (`CONTENT.md`
  §1–4).
- **Regulatory / data-protection (future threshold).** Moving from stateless tools
  to stored personal health data materially raises data-protection and security
  obligations. *Mitigation:* treat that transition as a deliberate, resourced
  step with proper data architecture and consent — not something to drift across.
- **Wellbeing risk in engagement mechanics.** Comparison and gamification can harm
  vulnerable users. *Mitigation:* positive-frame-only design, flattering/segmented
  comparison, graceful streaks, harm-signal nudges, and no appearance-based
  features (see `ROADMAP.md` guardrails).
- **Platform dependence** (ad networks, Google algorithm). *Mitigation:* build an
  owned audience (email, returning users) and diversified revenue so no single
  platform is existential.
